Shilpa Sundar, PharmD, MPS

PhD Researcher  ·  Biomedical AI & Knowledge Graphs  ·  UNC Chapel Hill

Shilpa is a PhD researcher at UNC Chapel Hill, working in the Translational and Integrative Sciences Lab (TISLab) on biomedical knowledge graphs and graph deep learning. Her current focus is rare disease drug repurposing and drug-drug interaction prediction.

Before the PhD, she spent over five years as a healthcare consultant and commercial data analyst at Clarivate Analytics and Trinity Life Sciences, advising pharma and MedTech clients across EU, US, and JAPAC markets on pricing, market access, and forecasting. That clinical and commercial background still shapes how she approaches modeling — she cares about whether methods hold up against messy biomedical data, not just on a benchmark.

Other notes: 2023 Penn Healthcare Case Competition winner (Wharton), 2024 Nucleate RTP Finalist, and a participant in the 2025 AWS Open Science Hackathon on Knowledge Graphs.

Projects

RotatE Knowledge Graph Embeddings

Took the RotatE knowledge graph embedding model and applied TracInCP influence scoring to figure out which training subgraphs the model is actually relying on. The point is interpretability — when a KG embedding makes a prediction, you want to know which training facts pushed it that way, not just trust the score.

Biomedical KG Relevance Scoring

A scoring rubric for the 91 primary knowledge sources that feed into ROBOKOP-KG and RTX-KG2, built as part of the Every Cure Matrix project. Each source gets evaluated on four criteria and tagged HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW relevance — so anyone downstream can actually justify the sources they're pulling into a drug repurposing pipeline.

NLP Sentiment Analysis — BERT & Transformers

Fine-tuned BERT, DistilBERT, RoBERTa, and BERTweet on the Kaggle NLP Sentiment Analysis XM competition (4-class tweet sentiment). Added one improvement at a time — LR scheduling, early stopping, cross-validation — to see what each was actually worth. The underlying question: does pretraining on 850M+ tweets (BERTweet) beat a general-purpose transformer here?

Binary Rainfall Prediction — 14-Model ML Pipeline

14-model comparison for Kaggle Playground Series S5E3 (binary rainfall prediction). Worked up from single classifiers to voting ensembles, stratified K-fold ensembles, and finally two-layer stacking — each step compared against the previous one. The two-layer stack with an LGBM meta-learner trained on out-of-fold predictions ended up giving the best AUC-ROC.

Comparative DDI Prediction Benchmark

A side-by-side benchmark of GNN-based DDI prediction models — vanilla GNNs, SumGNN, KGNN, and EmerGNN — across molecular structures, pharmacological networks, and biomedical knowledge graphs. The aim is to figure out which approach actually generalizes, not just which scores highest on one dataset. Manuscript in prep.

Legal Interoperability in Biomedical KGs

A licensing audit of 39 biomedical data sources, treating legal constraints as actual FAIR infrastructure rather than something you deal with at the end. Each source ends up in one of five operational reuse categories based on license type, commercial permissions, redistribution rights, and ShareAlike obligations. Manuscript in prep.
