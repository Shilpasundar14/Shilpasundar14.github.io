PhD Researcher  ·  Biomedical AI & Knowledge Graphs  ·  UNC Chapel Hill

Shilpa Sundar is a PhD researcher at UNC Chapel Hill with a concentration in Computational Knowledge Engineering, Health Informatics, and Genetics. Her research at the Translational and Integrative Sciences Lab (TISLab) focuses on graph-based deep learning, biomedical knowledge graphs, and scalable ML systems — with applications in rare disease drug repurposing and drug-drug interaction prediction.

With 5+ years of experience as a healthcare consultant and commercial data analyst at Clarivate Analytics and Trinity Life Sciences, she brings clinical domain expertise, market strategy, and applied ML to complex biomedical problems — consulting for pharma and MedTech clients across EU, US, and JAPAC markets on pricing, market access, and data-driven forecasting.

Recent highlights: Winner of the 2023 Penn Healthcare Case Competition (Wharton), Nucleate RTP Finalist (2024), and participant in the NVIDIA-sponsored AWS Open Science Hackathon on Knowledge Graphs (2025).

Biomedical KG Relevance Scoring

A reproducible scoring rubric to evaluate 91 primary knowledge sources across ROBOKOP-KG and RTX-KG2 for AI-aided drug repurposing. Each source is scored on four criteria and assigned HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW relevance, enabling principled source selection for downstream ML. Part of the Every Cure Matrix project.

NLP Sentiment Analysis — BERT & Transformers

Fine-tuned BERT, DistilBERT, RoBERTa, and BERTweet for 4-class tweet sentiment classification on the Kaggle NLP Sentiment Analysis XM competition. Systematically layered improvements — learning rate scheduling, early stopping, cross-validation — to isolate each technique's contribution. Evaluates whether domain-specialized pretraining (BERTweet: 850M+ tweets) outperforms general-purpose transformers.

Binary Rainfall Prediction — 14-Model ML Pipeline

A systematic ML experiment comparing 14 models — single classifiers, voting ensembles, stratified K-fold ensembles, and two-layer stacking — for binary rainfall prediction (Kaggle Playground Series S5E3). Each added layer of complexity is evaluated against the one before it. Meta-model stacking with an LGBM meta-learner trained on out-of-fold predictions yielded the best AUC-ROC generalization.

Legal Interoperability in Biomedical KGs

A systematic licensing audit of 39 biomedical data sources, treating legal constraints as first-class FAIR infrastructure. Each source is classified into one of five operational reuse categories based on license type, commercial permissions, redistribution rights, and ShareAlike obligations. This work is ongoing; a manuscript is currently in preparation.
